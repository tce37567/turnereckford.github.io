Spring Flowers

Types Daffodil Tulip Puschkinia Crocus Bluebell
Origins Spain and Protugal Central Asia Caucasus and Middle East Greece Scotland


Daffodils are the “cheeriest” of spring bulbs and the most popular. Daffodils are most common in the color yellow, but they also come in white, cream, orange, and pink. What makes the more popular is the low maintenance that they require.

Tulips are considered to be the most colorful of the spring flowers. Along with Daffodils, they are low maintenance and very easy to grow. Tulips also come in almost every color.

Another name for Puschkinia flowers is Striped Squill. The maintenance is also quite low for these flowers, making them popular during the spring time. These bulbs are white with a blue stripe going down the middle of each peddle and they usually grow in clumps of 8 or 10.

The Crocus flower is also considered a cheery flower because it grows in a variety of colors like purple, yellow, lavender, cream, and white. They typically have a bright orange center full of pollen that the bees love.

Bluebells grow in wooded areas and are considered a wildflower. Hummingbirds love this type of flower because of its shape. Bluebells most commonly grow in a bright blue color but can also be found in white and rarely pink.
